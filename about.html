About Us

1986

The Okan Foundation for Culture, Education, and Sports was established in 1986 with the objective of conducting research and studies in the social, cultural, and economic fields, focusing on issues related to sports, health, and education. The foundation aims to prepare proposals for the resolution of these issues, offering suggestions for solutions within the integrity of the country, and assisting the government in their implementation.

1999

Istanbul Okan University, one of Turkey's youngest and most dynamic universities, was officially established by the Okan Foundation for Culture, Education, and Sports in 1999.

2003

In the academic year 2003-2004, Istanbul Okan University commenced its educational activities. During this period, the Faculty of Economics and Administrative Sciences, Faculty of Arts and Sciences, and Faculty of Engineering and Architecture were established. Additionally, the Institutes of Social Sciences and Natural Sciences were founded within the same academic year. In the inaugural year, the university achieved notable success, with students entering its programs ranking among the top 1100 candidates. Istanbul Okan University secured a position among the top 7 foundation universities. In its founding year, the university admitted a total of 87 students across various departments: 23 students in the English Translation and Interpreting Department (4 on scholarship, 19 tuition-paying), 18 students in the Psychology Department (5 on scholarship, 13 tuition-paying), 26 students in the Business Administration Department (5 on scholarship, 21 tuition-paying), and 18 students in the Tourism and Hotel Management School (5 on scholarship, 13 tuition-paying). Identifying itself with the slogan "The University Closest to Business Life" since its establishment, Istanbul Okan University has provided students with numerous internship opportunities and has engaged in collaborative projects with industry partners such as Türk Telekom A.Ş. and DEİK (Foreign Economic Relations Board). The Continuing Education Center, OKSEM, became operational in January 2004.

2004

In the academic year 2004-2005, Istanbul Okan University expanded its academic offerings by introducing programs in German Translation and Interpreting, Russian Translation and Interpreting, Industrial Engineering, and International Trade. During the same academic year, the university gained popularity among students, with its programs being chosen by those ranking within the top 400 in Turkey. It further distinguished itself by securing the 3rd position among universities admitting students based on language proficiency, with candidates within the top 2000 in language scores. On June 3, 2005, the Faculty of Fine Arts was inaugurated as a new addition to the university's academic departments.

2006

Starting from the academic year 2006-2007, our rapidly growing university relocated to the new and modern Tuzla Campus. Distinguished by its contemporary architecture, the Tuzla Campus possesses all the features essential for a modern university. It is equipped with modern classrooms, computer and technical laboratories, a library, conference halls, sports centers, cafeterias, and restaurants to ensure students receive education in the most efficient manner. The campus offers additional conveniences with its proximity to Sabiha Gökçen Airport and the F1 Circuit.

2007

Operating under the slogan "The University Closest to Business Life," Istanbul Okan University graduated its first class in the 2006-2007 academic year. With its expert academic staff and a contemporary approach to education, the university has been preparing its students for professional life from the very beginning, integrating theory and practice through practical-oriented studies. It successfully realized its claim by placing all its graduates into the workforce. In 2007, Istanbul Okan University opened the School of Applied Sciences, which began admitting students. In the 2007-2008 academic year, a new academic building was inaugurated, and the course "Business World and Entrepreneurship" was added to the business administration department curriculum. Aiming to build a bridge between education and the business world, Istanbul Okan University facilitates its students' involvement in businesses as part of the Business Life Preparation Program starting from the first year. This allows students to familiarize themselves with the professional environment, establish a connection between theory and practice, and enhance their knowledge, skills, and motivation. Students who successfully complete the Business Life Preparation Program receive a participation certificate, and upon graduation, certificates related to work experience are added to their diplomas.

2008

In the Social Center, which became operational in 2008, there are amenities such as a pocket cinema, sauna, cafeterias, cocktail lounge, shopping center, and club rooms. To support students in their personal development, a 400-person sports center was opened in the Life Center building on our campus. The sports center, equipped with a fitness gym, swimming pool, tennis court, and fields for basketball, volleyball, and soccer, is designed to help students make the most of their leisure time. In the 2008-2009 academic year, the Law Faculty and Vocational School started education with a total of 29 departments in both regular and evening classes. Our university has adopted a unique structure by implementing a shared program that is only available in three universities. This allows students to save time when choosing their desired departments. As a pioneering move that would later grow into significant dimensions in the field of health, the School of Health Sciences was established on August 18, 2009, at Istanbul Okan University.

2010

In the academic years 2009-2010, the construction of the Faculty of Engineering and Architecture building, as well as the completion of the Faculty of Fine Arts and the School of Applied Sciences buildings, marked the commencement of education in these new facilities. Becoming a Chinese and Russian exam center, Istanbul Okan University continued its international initiatives by signing collaboration protocols with Franklin University, Ball State University, and three Chinese universities. During the 2009-2010 academic period, our university graduated its fourth set of alumni. Additionally, a social responsibility project supported by our university gained significant public support. The "Sınav Günlüğü" program on CNN Türk, carried out within this year, opened up new horizons for many students.

2011

On December 19, 2011, Istanbul Okan University inaugurated Istanbul's first Russian Cultural Center within its Tuzla Campus in a ceremony. In June 2011, the university conducted the test launch of an Autonomous Driverless Vehicle on the Tuzla Campus, successfully completing its initial rounds. On October 26, 2011, the Faculty of Education was established at Istanbul Okan University.

2013

On June 13-14, 2012, Istanbul Okan University hosted the second International Finance Congress, where the goals and strategies for Istanbul to become a financial center were discussed. In 2012, Istanbul Okan University initiated the operation of the Institute of Health Sciences to conduct master's and doctoral programs, and the School of Health Services to offer associate degree programs. In June 2011, the university conducted the test launch of an Autonomous Driverless Vehicle on the Tuzla Campus, successfully completing its initial rounds. On October 26, 2011, the Faculty of Education was established at Istanbul Okan University.

2013

On September 16, 2013, following the opening ceremony of the 2013-2014 Academic Year held at the Tuzla Campus, a groundbreaking ceremony took place for Turkey's first Medical and Health Sciences Education Complex, which would host the faculties of Medicine and Dentistry. On December 16, 2013, Istanbul Okan University officially opened the Faculty of Medicine and Dentistry for education and training.

2014

In the 2014-2015 academic year, Istanbul Okan University continued its educational activities with 8 faculties (Faculty of Medicine, Faculty of Dentistry, Faculty of Education, Faculty of Law, Faculty of Engineering and Architecture, Faculty of Economics and Administrative Sciences, Faculty of Fine Arts, Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences), 2 schools (School of Health Sciences, School of Applied Sciences), and 2 vocational schools (School of Health Services, Vocational School). The university has made history as the first Turkish university with ISO 27001 International Information Security Standard, aiming to protect the confidentiality and integrity of information on its campuses and ensure its continuous accessibility. On the 15th anniversary of its founding, Istanbul Okan University proudly inaugurated the Faculty of Dentistry Education and Application Hospital. During the 2014-2015 academic year, the Faculty of Dentistry, along with the Faculty of Medicine, admitted its first students. The Faculty of Dentistry, with its distinguished academic staff and state-of-the-art educational infrastructure, has contributed to the momentum of health education.

2015

In the 2015-2016 Academic Year, Istanbul Okan University expanded its student accommodation facilities on the Tuzla Campus with the addition of the Meral Okan Student Dormitory. This new dormitory, consisting of two buildings for female students and two for male students, increased the total capacity of student dormitories on the Tuzla Campus to accommodate 1500 students. The dormitories are equipped with study rooms, dining halls, cafeterias, cleaning services, laundry facilities, a security team ensuring round-the-clock safety, a medical center with a continuous doctor presence, and both wired and wireless internet systems. In the same year, Istanbul Okan University also opened a conservatory and laid the foundation for the Istanbul Okan University Faculty of Medicine Education and Research Hospital.

2016

Opened in November 2016, Istanbul Okan University Hospital is situated on 50,000 square meters of closed area. With a capacity of 250 beds, the hospital includes 10 operating rooms, 47 adult and intensive care units, an IVF unit, an invasive radiology unit, as well as clinics and outpatient departments. Istanbul Okan University Hospital provides students with opportunities for theoretical lessons and practical applications in patient examination and treatment, both before and after graduation.

2017

In 2017, the Faculty of Dentistry Hospital was inaugurated in Tuzla, offering services in a 8,000 square meter facility with 140 units, equipped with state-of-the-art technology. Additionally, an Acıbadem Polyclinic was established in a 2,200 square meter area under the Istanbul Okan University Hospital. The School of Applied Sciences, opened in 2008, transformed into the Faculty of Applied Sciences in 2018.

2018

Bekir Okan Art, Culture, and Congress Center, featuring a multi-purpose conference hall with a capacity of 1550 people, a 4-story library, and an exhibition hall, was inaugurated.
